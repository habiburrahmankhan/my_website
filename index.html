Abstractive Text Summarization

Abstractive text summarizers with sequence to sequence models have become quite popular these days. In this project, we study abstractive text summarization models such as LSTM (encoder-decoder) with attention mechanism. These models have two problems: they may generate factually incorrect details, and at times they repeat themselves. Hence, we try to overcome the problems associated with these models by using Pointer Generator networks and Coverage mechanisms. We will use a hybrid pointer generator network to copy words from the original text via pointing. This would enable an accurate representation of information without affecting the ability to generate new words through the generator. Next, we will try to keep a record of the text that has been summarized using the coverage mechanism. This record would enable us to overcome the repetition problem. With these improvements, we aim to build a model that would perform better than the previous model. The CNN/Daily Mail (news) dataset which is available online will be used. We will evaluate the models with ROGUE scores (a set of metrics for evaluating automatic summarization of texts).
